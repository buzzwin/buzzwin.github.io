Welcome to UT Austin, Son!

I'm so proud of you for stepping into the world of computer science. It's a fun, challenging, and meaningful journey. I've put together a few resources to help you get started—some for inspiration, some for learning, and some just to stay in the loop.

"The best way to learn is by doing—and staying curious." — Dad

Communities & News

  • Hacker News: A pulse on the tech industry—startups, breakthroughs, and debates.
  • UT Austin Subreddit: For campus life, advice, and tips from fellow students.
  • UT Austin CS Discord: A great place to connect, ask for help, and share cool stuff.
  • InfoQ: For when you're ready to learn how professionals think and build software at scale.

Learning & Practice

  • GitHub: Create, share, and collaborate on code. It’s your online coding portfolio.
  • Stack Overflow: Ask questions. Answer questions. Learn faster.
  • freeCodeCamp: Practice coding and build real-world projects step by step.
  • Coursera: Optional classes that can supplement your coursework.
  • CS50 by Harvard: A legendary intro course if you ever want to revisit the basics with clarity.

Words from Dad

CS can be overwhelming at first, but don’t stress about knowing everything right away. Focus on building things, one small project at a time. Your curiosity, kindness, and grit will take you farther than any algorithm.

I’m always here if you want to talk shop—or just take a break and grab a taco.
